"domain_profile": "football-v1" and a "domain_analysis" object to the generic JSON

Keep dimensions separate. A report can criticize a referee, support a team, use fan
emotion, and contain tactical analysis simultaneously. Use primary_stance for the most
central expressed position and secondary_stances for other explicit positions.

Do not treat a referee decision, federation explanation, pundit view, or fan claim as
independently verified. Classify accusations of rigging, corruption, secret plans, or
intentional favoritism as allegations/conspiracy framing unless the supplied text
contains direct evidence. Extract attributed opinions only when a named person or
organization and its view appear in the text; never invent credentials or consensus.
Set is_direct_source to true only for the speaker's own statement, interview,
transcript, or authored analysis; an outlet repeating a quote is not a direct source.
Use source_role=unknown when the role is not explicit. Keep incident_ref specific and
consistent with the incident description. Position describes the speaker's judgment,
not the article's stance. Use null and empty arrays for information not present.
For a first-person social post, the supplied author may identify the speaker and
source_role may be fan when the source_type is social; do not infer professional
credentials from a username, subreddit, popularity, or engagement counts.
